The Arrowsmith Program

A scientifically-proven approach to strengthening cognitive capacities and transforming the way you learn.

[Photo: Barbara Arrowsmith-Young]

Barbara Arrowsmith-Young

Barbara Arrowsmith-Young is the founder and director of Arrowsmith School and the Arrowsmith Program. Her own journey of overcoming severe learning disabilities led her to develop this groundbreaking program.

Born with multiple learning disabilities, Barbara spent years struggling in school. Through her research into neuroplasticity, she developed cognitive exercises that changed her own brain—and her life. Since 1978, she has dedicated herself to helping others do the same.

Her book, "The Woman Who Changed Her Brain", tells her remarkable story and has inspired thousands around the world.

Program Components

The program uses various types of exercises to target different cognitive functions.

Computer Exercises

Interactive software exercises that target specific cognitive functions, with progressive levels of difficulty.

Audio Exercises

Listening-based activities that improve auditory processing and comprehension skills.

Reading & Writing

Structured exercises that strengthen the cognitive processes underlying literacy skills.

Individual Program Outcomes

Students experience real, measurable improvements across many areas.

Improved Handwriting

Better motor symbol sequencing leads to clearer, more fluent handwriting and sentence structure.

Faster Processing Speed

Students can take in and respond to information more quickly and accurately.

Better Reading Comprehension

Improved ability to understand and retain what is read.

Enhanced Memory

Stronger working memory and ability to remember information.

Logical Reasoning

Better problem-solving and understanding of cause and effect.

Increased Confidence

As learning becomes easier, self-esteem and motivation grow.
