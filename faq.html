Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the questions we get asked most often about the Arrowsmith Program and A1 Student.

The Arrowsmith Program is a cognitive training program designed to strengthen the weak cognitive capacities underlying learning difficulties. Rather than working around learning problems with accommodations, it addresses the root cause by using targeted exercises based on the principles of neuroplasticity—the brain's ability to change and grow.

Developed by Barbara Arrowsmith-Young over 40 years ago, the program has helped thousands of students worldwide overcome learning challenges including dyslexia, ADHD, dyscalculia, and other learning differences.

The Arrowsmith Program is designed for anyone aged 7 and up who struggles with learning. This includes:

  • Children and teenagers with learning difficulties
  • Students diagnosed with dyslexia, ADHD, dyscalculia, or other learning differences
  • Adults who want to improve their cognitive function for career or personal reasons
  • Anyone who feels they're not reaching their full learning potential

The length of the program varies depending on the individual's cognitive profile and how many areas need strengthening. Most students are in the program for 2-4 years, though some complete it sooner and others may take longer.

The important thing is that changes are permanent—once a cognitive capacity is strengthened, it stays strong. This is different from tutoring or accommodations that must be maintained indefinitely.

Results vary by individual, but students typically see improvements in:

  • Reading speed and comprehension
  • Writing and handwriting quality
  • Mathematical reasoning
  • Memory and recall
  • Processing speed
  • Attention and focus
  • Self-confidence and motivation

Many parents report that their child goes from struggling every day to actually enjoying learning.

Yes. The Arrowsmith Program is based on over 40 years of research into neuroplasticity. Multiple studies, including brain imaging research, have shown measurable changes in brain structure and function after completing the program.

Tutoring helps students learn specific content by working around their learning difficulties. It addresses symptoms but not the underlying cause.

The Arrowsmith Program is different—it strengthens the cognitive capacities that make learning difficult in the first place. Once these are strengthened, students can learn more efficiently across all subjects, without ongoing support.
